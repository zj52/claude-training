## Cornell adaptation notes

### What the quiz tells us about must-stick takeaways

The quiz is a useful filter — Anthropic chose these 10 graded items as the ones every learner should walk away with. That maps neatly onto Module 2's job: don't try to re-teach everything, just make sure these stick.

The takeaways, grouped:

**Framework anchors (Q1, Q2, Q3)**
- The 4 D's and the two loops are foundational. Module 2's slide 2 (The two loops) covers Q1 + Q2 in one breath.
- The three types of Diligence (Q3) are a more granular point — worth a callout when we get to Diligence on slide 3 or in the "stand behind every word" slide (10).

**Decision rules (Q4, Q5)**
- *"Should AI do this?"* not *"Can AI do this?"* (Q4) — already a memorable phrase in the plan. Worth making it the slide-5 title (the task-type filter slide). Direct lift.
- "Review terms and conditions" (Q5) — the *generic* answer. Cornell-specific version: *"Cornell has reviewed the terms for you; use the approved tool for the right data class."* Slide 4 (Green/Yellow/Red) already addresses this; Cornell's answer is *easier* than the generic one.

**Definition (Q6)**
- *"Efficient, effective, ethical, safe"* — the definition of AI Fluency. Worth lifting verbatim into Module 2's opening slide (slide 2) or making it the workshop's tagline.

**Identity / values (Q7)**
- *"Ensuring AI serves your mission and maintaining the relationships that define your work."* For research admin: *ensuring AI serves the research mission and maintaining the PI/sponsor/colleague relationships that define our work.* Worth a slide of its own — possibly the closing-slide framing for slide 11 ("What you can do you couldn't before") or slide 12 (homework + Module 3 preview).

**Practical habits (Q8, Q9, Q10)**
- Q8 (Discernment on drafts) → already covered in slide 10 ("stand behind every word")
- Q9 (Upload past successful work) → already covered in slide 12 bridge to Cowork ("selected folder makes this automatic")
- Q10 (Test against known data) → already the centerpiece of slide 9 (hands-on exercise)

### Recommended additions / lift-ins to Module 2 slides

Three concrete updates worth making:

1. **Slide 2 (The two loops)** — incorporate the definition from Q6 verbatim: *"AI Fluency: the ability to use AI in ways that are efficient, effective, ethical, and safe."* As a subtitle or anchor line.
2. **Slide 5 title** — adopt Q4's phrasing: *"'Should AI do this?' — not 'Can AI do this?'"*
3. **"Human in the loop" callout** — Q7's framing deserves explicit screen-time. Two options:
   - Add a small "Human in the loop" anchor inside slide 10 (stand behind every word) — they're closely related ideas.
   - Or make it its own slide between 10 and 11. The room may need a moment to land *why* we're doing all this.

### A useful pedagogical note

A few of the quiz questions could be opened as *room polls* in Module 2 — surface where the cohort is before we teach the answer. Q4 ("Should vs. Can") and Q10 ("How do you build confidence in AI's data analysis?") are particularly good for this: they have a clear right answer the room may not have seen yet, so the *aha* moment is worth the 30 seconds. Worth piloting in the live workshop if the format allows.
