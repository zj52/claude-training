## Title-slide content (from the resource sync — first slide only)

- Title: **AI Fluency**
- Subtitle: *Based on material from Anthropic*
- Context: *AI Training · Cornell · Today*
- Anchor line: *"AI Fluency means engaging with AI systems effectively, efficiently, ethically, and safely."*
- The 4D glosses (one-line per D) as listed on the title slide:
  - **Delegation:** thoughtfully deciding what work to do with AI vs. doing yourself
  - **Description:** communicating clearly with AI systems
  - **Discernment:** evaluating AI outputs and behavior with a critical eye
  - **Diligence:** ensuring you interact with AI responsibly
- Additional slide topics flagged in the sync excerpt (worth investigating in the full deck):
  - "Traditional [AI] analyzes and classifies existing data; Gen AI creates something new."
  - "AI's Working Memory — Context Window"

## Wording note

The deck's *"engaging with AI systems effectively, efficiently, ethically, and safely"* is a slight rewording of the AIF-NP course's *"the ability to use AI in ways that are efficient, effective, ethical, and safe."* Both work; the course version is what we plan to quote on Module 2's slide 2 (per the quiz anchor — see `course-content/AIF-NP/10-quiz.md`). Worth choosing one and using it consistently.

## Topics in this deck that AREN'T in the AIF-NP course

- **Traditional vs. Generative AI** — a foundational framing that doesn't appear explicitly in AIF-NP. Could belong in Module 1 (Foundations) or as an opener in Module 2.
- **Context Window** — likewise not in AIF-NP, but extremely relevant for Cornell users who'll work with Claude 3P on long documents (proposal drafts, sponsor RFPs, multi-document analysis). This is a Module 1 slide candidate or Module 2 enrichment.

Both are useful additions worth preserving from the existing Cornell deck when we build Module 2.
