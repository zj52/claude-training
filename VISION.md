# Vision — Cornell Claude Training

*The "why" underneath the workshop. The line every module should land.*

---

## You are the agent.

The training exists to give research administrators the **agency to define how they work** — to encode their playbooks for routine work and free up time to strategize, make decisions, orchestrate, and build the relationships needed to deliver on Cornell's research mission.

Pieces of what we do can be encoded in these skills and plugins with access to tools, shared amongst our teams, and made better.

**Claude gives us a platform to do this and to connect with our existing systems.**

---

## How the vision shapes the workshop

This is the *why* underneath the *Theory → Policy → Operational Tooling* arc. Each module advances the vision in a specific way:

- **Module 1 (Foundations)** establishes that Claude 3P is a tool you can trust with the right data — the precondition for treating yourself as the agent who configures it.
- **Module 2 (AI Fluency)** gives you the framework and the **policy** that defines how *your* work happens. The unit-level AI policy is, in effect, your declaration of agency: *here's how my team uses AI; here's what stays human.*
- **Module 3 (Cowork)** turns that policy into operational tooling. Your playbooks become skills. The cohort's skills get refined with RAIS and the AI Innovation Hub, roll up into a shared library, and eventually become Cornell-specific plugins. The work compounds.

## How the vision shapes what we say

A few formulations the deck can return to:

- *"AI isn't replacing research administrators — it's giving each of you agency over the routine so you can do more of the work that actually requires you."*
- *"Encode the playbook. Free the judgment."*
- *"You own the playbook. The tool runs it. RAIS and the AI Innovation Hub help you sharpen it."*
- *"Pieces of what we do, encoded into skills with access to tools, shared across teams, and made better."*
